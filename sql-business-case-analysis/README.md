# Sql-business-case-analysis
This project focuses on answering common business questions using SQL on transcational sales data.
The analysis demonstrates how SQL can be used to evaluate revenue performance, customer contribution, sales trend to support data-driven decision making.

# Business Questions
1. What is the overall revenue generated?
2. What is the average order value?
3. Which categories contributes the most revenue?
4. Which customers generates the highest revenue?
5. How does revenue trend over time?
6. Which categories have high order volume but relatively low revenue?

# Dataset
- Transaction sales dataset sourced from kaggle.
- Includes order level data such as order value, date, customer and category.
- Dataset was scoped to focus on revenue and customer analysis.

# Tools Used
- SQL: Data aggregation and business analysis.

# Key Analysis
- Calculated total revenue and average order value.
- Analyzed revenue contribution by category and customer.
- Evaluated sales trend over time.
- Identified high frequency but low revenue categories.

# Key Takeaways
- Revenue is led by one dominant category, while the remaining categories show relatively similar performance, indicating a concentrated but competitve distribution.
- A small group of customers contribute a disproportiante share of total revenue, highlighting customer concentration risk.
- Revenue trends fluctuate over time but maintain an overall stable pattern, suggesting predictable sales behavior with periodic volatility.
- Some categories exhibit high order volume but lower revenue contribution, indicating potential pricing or product mix inefficiencies.
